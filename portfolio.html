My Portfolio

Some colorful highlights in my work or personal projects.

  • DSG Storeboard

    I designed and built a metrics display dashboard for store associates to track performance stats as part of my internship with Dick's Sporting Goods.

  • CMU Teknowledge

    I develop curriculum teaching young kids the basics of computation through Python with the student organization Teknowledge.

  • Women@SCS

    I work on redesigning and maintaining the new Women@SCS website. I'm also part of the committee that plans and organizes club events.

  • Dance

    I started learning hip-hop dance with the student organizations Dancers' Symposium and KPDC, performing with them on a semester basis.

  • Branch Out Education

    In 2016, I cofounded the organization Branch Out with my friend and colleague Shreya Parjan, bringing educational opportunities to young innovators across all backgrounds. We launched our pilot program, the Innovation Lab, that summer.

  • Origami

    LinkedIn meets Instagram: a social networking platform for students and young professionals to find opportunities and connect with peers. Developed for web and Android

  • CU Hacks

    As part of Cupertino Library's Teen Advisory Board, I helped plan and organize its second and third annual teen Hackathons.

  • Inertia Simulator

    I created a more lightweight, efficient version of Raman Pfaff's moment of inertia lab simulation using Processing and JavaScript to aid with concepts covered in Physics C: Mechanics. Developed for web.

  • Branch App

    For the 3rd annual Astra STEAM Summit, my team won first place for our app Branch. Branch aids immigrants throughout the moving process in preparing for and adjusting to their new living environments.

  • Code for Fun

    The summer of 2015 I volunteered with Code for Fun, a nonprofit dedicated to teaching young students the basics of coding. I helped with Python, Game Design, and Product Design camps.

  • Voluntweer

    An app to connect teens to volunteer opportunities. Made for the 2014-15 Technovation competition. Developed for Android.

  • KeysandTimers

    A game to learn how to make games, teaching, as the name suggests, the usage of Timers and KeyListeners in Java.

  • Piano

    I've played piano for 11 years and passed the 10 levels of the California Merit exam. No longer a huge part of my life but hey - I can really pound out some Mary Had a Little Lamb.

  • Fresh Catch

    An app for small-scale fishermen that helps address the issue of mislabelled fish products. Made for the 2014 Monterey Bay Aquarium Fishackathon. Developed for iOS.

  • Skateboarding

    I recently started teaching myself to skateboard. Use it mostly to get around quickly, but hope to one day master a couple of tricks!
